There are many ways to map an election, and each form represents a different kind of truth.

The default view shows your typical map, and it emphasizes the winners of each county, colored darker by how big the win is. Given that this election was won by Joe Biden, you may find it surprising. While this form is truthful in the way that it represents the winner, it under-emphasizes how many people cast their votes in a given county.

Which bring us to representing each county as a graduated (???) circle. This fairly emphasizes the amount of votes each candidate got by tying it to a circle's area. What we lose, however, is the true area of each county.

Another way is to create a choropleth where it is colored not by how much one party beat the other, but by how many votes were cast in total in that county.
